About Me

I am a final-year Computer Science undergraduate at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore. My research lies at the intersection of language models, acoustic AI, medical imaging, and control systems, with a focus on building AI systems that are both dependable and imaginative. Currently, at NTU, I am thankful to be supervised by Dr. Chng Eng Siong and Dr. Rao Wei for my final-year project on avian acoustic monitoring. I am also grateful to be working in Dr. Alvin Guo Wei Chanâ€™s lab, where I explore language model hallucinations and creativity.

In acoustic AI, I was fortunate to be advised by Srikanth Nagisetty during my internship at Panasonic R&D Singapore. During this internship, I developed ReCoOP, a stereo SELD framework that combined ResNet-Conformers with ONE-PEACE embeddings. The system outperformed the baseline by 17.8% in F-score, placed 3rd globally in the IEEE AASP DCASE 2025 Challenge, and was accepted at the 10th DCASE Workshop. Additionally, I also built a dysarthric speech detection pipeline using Wav2Vec2 with CNN-Attention, deployed as a real-time Streamlit app. In my Final Year Project, I am extending these ideas to ecology by building a low-latency pipeline on the BirdSet dataset for bird vocalization detection, diarization, and classification, working within a 7-member team and targeting edge deployment.

In medical imaging, I was selected as one of 57 students worldwide for the MBZUAI UGRIP 2025 program, where I was grateful to work under Dr. Mohammad Yaqub, Dr. Hu Wang, and Dr. Mostafa Salem as part of a six-member international team from six different countries. I co-developed MISFIT, a two-stage wavelet-domain framework for MRI modality synthesis, and contributed to an end-to-end neuro-oncology pipeline spanning synthesis of missing MRIs, segmentation of brain tumors in low-resource Sub-Saharan Africa data, and classification of tumor responses. Our team received the Best Team Award within UGRIP, and the works were published in the MICCAI 2025 BraTS Challenge proceedings.

Earlier, as part of NTU's URECA Program, I was thankful to be supervised by Dr. Christopher Ho Tin Lee and Zhao Chenhaoa on motor drive control, where I designed a neural network compensator within an ADRC loop to suppress torque ripple in PMSMs. This project, which demonstrated the potential of data-driven control for real engineering systems, was presented at ICUR 2024.

Across these domains, my goal remains constant: to design AI systems that do not treat reliability and creativity as trade-offs, but instead make them reinforce each other. I am always excited to explore new collaborations and discussions in this space.
